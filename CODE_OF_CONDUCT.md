# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, as contributors and maintainers of the **Open Manuscript Initiative (OMI)**, are committed to fostering an open, welcoming, respectful, and inclusive community.

We believe that scholarly communication benefits from collaboration across disciplines, cultures, institutions, and countries. Every participant should feel safe to contribute ideas, ask questions, report issues, and participate in discussions without fear of harassment or discrimination.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment include:

- Being respectful and constructive.
- Welcoming different perspectives and experiences.
- Providing thoughtful and evidence-based feedback.
- Accepting constructive criticism gracefully.
- Focusing discussions on ideas rather than individuals.
- Supporting open scientific collaboration.
- Respecting intellectual property and proper attribution.
- Helping newcomers learn and contribute.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks.
- Discriminatory language or behavior.
- Trolling or deliberately disruptive conduct.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Plagiarism or intentional misrepresentation of scholarly work.
- Any behavior that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional research environment.

## Scholarly Integrity

OMI supports responsible research practices.

Contributors are expected to:

- cite sources appropriately;
- acknowledge the work of others;
- avoid plagiarism;
- report errors honestly;
- communicate transparently;
- respect research ethics and academic integrity.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories
- Discussions
- Issue trackers
- Pull requests
- Documentation
- Community meetings
- Any official communication channels related to OMI

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, documentation, issues, or other contributions that violate these guidelines.

Repeated or serious violations may result in temporary or permanent restrictions from participating in the project.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please contact the project maintainers through the project's GitHub repository.

Reports will be handled respectfully and, where possible, confidentially.
